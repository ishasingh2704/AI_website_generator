What is global warming?

Global warming is the long-term increase in Earth’s average surface temperature due to greenhouse gases like CO₂ from human activities. It drives climate change, affecting weather patterns, sea levels, and biodiversity.

Why it matters

Even small temperature changes intensify heatwaves, storms, droughts, and wildfires. Rising seas threaten coastal communities. Protecting the climate safeguards health, food, water, and a livable future for all.
